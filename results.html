From the above dimensions, we selected six dimensions – accuracy, completeness, consistency, timeliness, uniqueness, validity, since they appear across multiple frameworks, they are distinct from each other, and were deemed relevant for the open data to be evaluated. Also, since we will be considering open data some of the data dimensions would not be applicable such as Latency, Compliance, etc.. Although these selected dimensions are similar to the Data Management Association’s (DAMA) framework dimensions, they present a difference in how their metrics are measured or scored. Additionally, most of the existing research and implementations on data quality frameworks focus solely on closed data while our implementation focuses on open data quality assessment.
